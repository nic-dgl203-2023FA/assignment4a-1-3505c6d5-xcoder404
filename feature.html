Apple Pie

Apple pie is a beloved dessert made with a flaky pastry crust and a luscious filling of sliced apples, sugar, and warm spices like cinnamon and nutmeg. Baked to perfection, it's a classic comfort food, often served with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a delightful combination of sweet and tart flavors.

Ingredients

For the Pie crust:

  • 21/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1cup(2 sticks) unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 6-8 tablespoons ice water

For the Filling:

  • 6-7 cups of peeled, cored, and thinly sliced apples (such as Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, or a mix of varieties)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into small pieces

Intructions

  1. Prepare the Pie Crust:
    1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt.
    2. Add the cold, cubed butter to the flour mixture.
    3. Use a pastry cutter, fork, or your hands to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    4. Gradually add ice water, one tablespoon at a time, and mix just until the dough comes together.
    5. Divide the dough in half, shape each half into a disc, wrap in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat the Oven:
    1. Preheat the oven to 425
  3. Prepare the Filling:
    1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the sliced apples, granulated sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice. Toss until the apples are well coated.
  4. Roll Out the Pie Crust:
    1. Roll out one of the dough discs on a floured surface to fit your pie dish.
    2. Place the rolled-out dough into a 9-inch pie dish, leaving excess dough hanging over the edges.
  5. Add the Apple Filling:
    1. Pour the apple filling into the pie crust.
    2. Dot the filling with small pieces of butter.
  6. Roll Out the Second Crust:
    1. Roll out the second dough disc on a floured surface to create the top crust. You can leave it whole or cut it into strips for a lattice design.
  7. Cover the Pie:
    1. Place the second crust over the filling.
    2. Trim any excess dough, leaving about a 1/2-inch overhang.
    3. Fold the overhanging dough under the bottom crust and crimp the edges to seal the pie.
  8. Ventilation:
    1. If you're using a whole top crust, make small slits in the center to allow steam to escape. If you're making a lattice top, no need for slits.
  9. Bake:
    1. Place the pie in the preheated oven and bake for 45-55 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  10. Cool and Serve:
    1. Let the pie cool for at least a few hours before slicing to allow the filling to set. Serve with a scoop of vanilla ice cream for a delicious treat!
